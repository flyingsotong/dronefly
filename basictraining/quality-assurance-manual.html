1. Introduction and Scope

1.1 Purpose

This Quality Assurance Manual establishes the comprehensive framework for maintaining and continuously improving the quality of training services provided by Dronefly.sg as an Unmanned Aircraft Basic Training Organisation (UABTO). It serves as the foundational document that guides all quality-related activities, ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements and delivering exceptional educational outcomes.

1.2 Scope

This manual applies to all aspects of UABTO operations, including:

  • Course development and content creation
  • Instructional delivery and student assessment
  • Certification processes and record keeping
  • Personnel management and development
  • Student services and support systems
  • Compliance monitoring and regulatory adherence
  • Continuous improvement initiatives

1.3 Regulatory Framework

This manual is developed in accordance with:

  • CAAS Advisory Circular AC 101-3-1(4) - Approvals for Unmanned Aircraft Training
  • Singapore Air Navigation Act and associated regulations
  • International best practices for aviation training organizations
  • ISO 9001:2015 Quality Management Systems principles

1.4 Quality Management Philosophy

Our quality management approach is built on the Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) cycle, emphasizing continuous improvement, risk-based thinking, and student-centered service delivery. We are committed to exceeding regulatory requirements while maintaining operational efficiency and educational excellence.

9. Continuous Improvement Process

9.1 PDCA Cycle Implementation

Our continuous improvement process follows the Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) cycle, ensuring systematic and sustainable enhancement of all UABTO operations.

PLAN

  • • Identify improvement opportunities
  • • Set measurable objectives
  • • Develop action plans
  • • Allocate resources

DO

  • • Implement planned changes
  • • Execute improvement initiatives
  • • Document implementation process
  • • Collect performance data

CHECK

  • • Monitor results and performance
  • • Compare against objectives
  • • Analyze effectiveness
  • • Identify lessons learned

ACT

  • • Standardize successful changes
  • • Adjust unsuccessful initiatives
  • • Update procedures and training
  • • Plan next improvement cycle
