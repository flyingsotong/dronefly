The essentials

Key safety regulations for outdoor flying

These rules apply to all drone operators for outdoor flights, regardless of weight or purpose.

Do

Maintain visual line-of-sight

Always keep your drone where you can see it with your own eyes, not just on a screen.

Don't

Fly beyond visual line-of-sight (BVLOS)

Flying a drone where you cannot see it is strictly prohibited without an Operator and Class 1 Activity Permit.

Don't

Fly over 200ft (60m)

Do not fly higher than 200 feet above Mean Sea Level (not AGL) unless you have a specific permit.

Don't

Fly in restricted areas

Do not fly within 5km of an airport or airbase. Always check OneMap before flying.

Do

Respect privacy

Do not fly over private property or take photos/videos of people without their consent.

Upcoming

Use Remote ID

From 1 Dec 2025, all drones over 250g must have Broadcast Remote ID (B-RID), a digital license plate.

Common questions

Frequently asked questions

Registration is a two-step process for all drones over 250g. First, you must purchase a registration label (S$25) online or at designated SingPost offices. Second, you complete the registration online via the CAAS UA Portal. You will need to affix the label to a non-removable part of your drone (like the main body) and upload a photo. You must be at least 16 years old to register. If you sell or lose your drone, you must de-register it.

Broadcast Remote Identification (B-RID) is like a digital license plate for your drone. It transmits information like the drone's serial number, position, and the operator's location. Starting from 1 December 2025, all drones weighing over 250g must be equipped with B-RID to be flown outdoors in Singapore. This can be a built-in feature or an external module attached to the drone.

CAAS maintains an official list of approved B-RID modules and drone models that have compliant, built-in B-RID functionality. You should consult this list before purchasing a drone or a separate module to ensure it meets the technical requirements. You can view the official list via the link below.

View Approved B-RID Products List (PDF)

The UA Basic Training Certificate is required for anyone flying a drone between 1.5kg and 7kg for recreational or educational purposes. To get it, you must be at least 16 years old, complete a one-time online theory course, and pass an online theory test. The training is conducted by CAAS-approved organisations, including:

  • A-Star-Training & Consultancy Pte Ltd
  • Avetics Global Pte Ltd
  • DJI Academy
  • Garuda Robotics Pte Ltd
  • Nanyang Polytechnic
  • Singapore Polytechnic
  • TP Engineering Pte Ltd

The UAPL is a more advanced license required for anyone flying a drone for commercial purposes (regardless of weight) or for recreational/educational purposes if the drone is over 7kg. To obtain a UAPL, you must be at least 16 years old, pass a theory test administered by CAAS, and pass a practical assessment with an authorised flight examiner.
