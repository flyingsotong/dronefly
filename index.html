The essentials

Key safety regulations for outdoor flying

These rules apply to all drone operators for outdoor flights, regardless of weight or purpose.

Do

Maintain visual line-of-sight

Always keep your drone where you can see it with your own eyes, not just on a screen.

Don't

Fly beyond visual line-of-sight (BVLOS)

Flying a drone where you cannot see it is strictly prohibited without an Operator and Class 1 Activity Permit.

Don't

Fly over 200ft (60m)

Do not fly higher than 200 feet above mean sea level unless you have a specific permit.

Don't

Fly in restricted areas

Do not fly within 5km of an airport or airbase. Always check the OneMap app before flying.

Do

Respect privacy

Do not fly over private property or take photos/videos of people without their consent.

Don't

Fly over crowds or events

Avoid flying over groups of people. Flying at events with more than 50 people requires special permits.

Common questions

Frequently asked questions

The UA Basic Training Certificate is required for anyone flying a drone between 1.5kg and 7kg for recreational or educational purposes. To get it, you must be at least 16 years old, complete a one-time online theory course, and pass an online theory test. The training is conducted by CAAS-approved organisations.

The UAPL is a more advanced license required for anyone flying a drone for commercial purposes (regardless of weight) or for recreational/educational purposes if the drone is over 7kg. To obtain a UAPL, you must be at least 16 years old, pass a theory test administered by CAAS, and pass a practical assessment with an authorised flight examiner.

An Operator Permit is for the company or business and is valid for one year. CAAS assesses the company's safety procedures, drone airworthiness, and personnel competency before issuing it.

An Activity Permit is for a specific flight or block of flights. A Class 1 permit is for commercial operations and requires an Operator Permit. A Class 2 permit is for recreational flights under special conditions (e.g., above 200ft) and does not require an Operator Permit.

Generally, no. Registration is not required for drones flown exclusively indoors in a private space. However, the rules change if the indoor space is publicly accessible (like a shopping mall or stadium) or if the flight is for an event with more than 50 people. In those specific indoor cases, a UA Pilot Licence and permits may be required.

Tourist attractions like Marina Bay Sands, the Merlion, and Gardens by the Bay are located within restricted airspace and are often designated as "protected areas." Flying a drone in these locations is generally not allowed without special permits, which can be difficult to obtain for recreational purposes. A permit from the Singapore Police Force (SPF) may also be required. Always assume flying is prohibited at major tourist spots unless you have explicit permission.
