Question 6

Flying for recreational or educational purpose, in a restricted, danger or protected area or within 5km from aerodrome or above 200ft AMSL.
A Class 2 Activity Permit (AP) is required for UA activities conducted outdoors for recreational or educational purposes using a UA of 7 kilograms or below in total mass (or 25kg or below for recreational purposes). UAPL, Operator Permit, Class 1 Activity Permit

Up to 25kg
A Class A UAPL (Unmanned Aircraft Pilot Licence) allows the holder to operate any UA with a total mass that does not exceed 25 kg. For UAs with a total mass that exceeds 25 kg, a Class B UAPL is required.

13 questions (28%) - Stalls, stability, forces of flight, and aircraft performance

Question 2
When does an aeroplane stall?
Exceed angle of attack, airflow separates at the top of the wing
An aerodynamic stall occurs when the critical angle of attack of the wing is exceeded, leading to a loss of lift and the inability of the UA to produce enough lift to support its weight. Airflow separates at the top of the wing during a stall.
Question 4
Which circumstance causes aircraft performance to suffer?
High density altitude
Aircraft performance suffers when the air is less dense. Air is less dense under circumstances of low air pressure, high temperature, and high humidity. High density altitude correlates with less dense air, which reduces aircraft performance because less air flows over the wing, generating less lift.
Question 7
How does a powered-lift aircraft pitch forward?
Thrust > Drag
To increase forward speed or accelerate, thrust must be greater than drag. "Powered-lift" aircraft are heavier-than-air aircraft capable of vertical take-off and landing (VTOL) and low-speed flight, relying on engine-driven lift devices or thrust for lift during these regimes, and non-rotating aerofoils for horizontal flight.
Question 11
What happens when the center of gravity is not at the geometric center?
Endurance of UA will reduce as more input is required
The Center of Gravity (CG) location directly relates to longitudinal (pitch) and lateral (bank) stability. If the CG is too far forward or aft, it can cause the UA to drift, requiring added control input. This increased control input reduces the UA's endurance. For example, a forward CG can decrease pitch maneuverability and require a higher angle of attack, leading to higher drag and reduced endurance.
Question 21
In high-density areas,
Aircraft/drone generates more lift
In high air density locations, more air flows over the wing, allowing the aircraft to generate more lift, thus improving aircraft performance. High air density is generally associated with low temperature, high air pressure, low humidity, and low altitude.
Question 25
A UA aeroplane controls pitch around the:
Lateral axis
The Elevator (or stabilator) is the primary control surface responsible for changes in pitch, which rotates the aircraft about the lateral axis. The vertical axis controls yaw, and the longitudinal axis controls roll.
Question 26
What happens when a UA's Center of Gravity moves forward?
Stability increases and pitch manoeuvrability increases
A forward Center of Gravity (CG) leads to increased longitudinal stability but decreased pitch maneuverability. It also results in a lower cruise speed (due to a higher Angle of Attack) and a higher stall speed. It can also lead to longer take-off distances. Question 34
When does density altitude have the same reading as pressure altitude?
When temperature is 15°C
Density altitude equals pressure altitude when the temperature is at the International Standard Atmosphere (ISA) standard temperature of 15°C at sea level. Under these standard conditions, density altitude and pressure altitude readings will be the same.
Question 35
When disturbed, an aircraft's tendency not to return to equilibrium or to not continue in the disturbed displacement is called:
Unstable
An unstable aircraft has the tendency to continue moving away from its original attitude when disturbed, rather than returning to equilibrium. This is in contrast to stable aircraft (which return to equilibrium) and neutrally stable aircraft (which remain in the new position).
Question 36
When an unmanned aircraft (UA) is in straight and level flight at a constant airspeed:
Lift equals weight and thrust equals drag
In straight and level flight at constant airspeed, all forces are balanced. Lift equals weight (vertical forces) and thrust equals drag (horizontal forces). This represents equilibrium flight conditions.
Question 41
Longitudinal stability of an unmanned aircraft is enhanced by:
Moving the center of gravity forward
Longitudinal stability (pitch stability) is enhanced by moving the center of gravity forward. A forward CG increases the aircraft's tendency to return to its original pitch attitude when disturbed, improving stability but potentially reducing maneuverability.

3. Radio Control & Communication Systems

5 questions (11%) - Transmitter modes, frequency interference, and controller binding

‹
›
Question 1
Which is the Mode 1 transmitter?
Left stick controls throttle and yaw, right stick controls pitch and roll
Mode 1 transmitter configuration has the left stick controlling throttle (vertical movement) and yaw (horizontal movement), while the right stick controls pitch (vertical movement) and roll (horizontal movement). This is different from Mode 2, where throttle and yaw are on the left stick, but pitch and roll are reversed.
Question 12
How can frequency interference between the transmitter and receiver be reduced?
Use frequency hopping spread spectrum (FHSS) or direct sequence spread spectrum (DSSS)
Frequency interference can be reduced by using spread spectrum technologies like FHSS (Frequency Hopping Spread Spectrum) or DSSS (Direct Sequence Spread Spectrum). These technologies spread the signal across multiple frequencies, making the communication more resistant to interference.
Question 16
What is true about controller binding?
It is a one-time process to pair the transmitter and receiver
Controller binding is the process of pairing a specific transmitter with a specific receiver so they can communicate securely. This is typically a one-time setup process that establishes a unique communication link between the two devices.
Question 29
How does a controller (GCS) pair with a receiver?
Through binding process
A Ground Control Station (GCS) or controller pairs with a receiver through a binding process. This process establishes a secure communication link between the transmitter and receiver, ensuring that only the paired devices can communicate with each other.
Question 32
What is the common cause of RC signal loss?
Distance and obstacles
The most common causes of RC (Radio Control) signal loss are excessive distance between the transmitter and receiver, and physical obstacles that block or interfere with the radio signal. Other factors can include interference from other electronic devices and low battery power.

Question 9
I want to fly my FPV drone for fun using goggles alone in an outdoor fly-zone area. My FPV weighs 1kg. What do I need?
UABT and visual observer
For FPV (First Person View) flying using goggles alone, you need a UA Basic Training Certificate (UABT) since the drone weighs 1kg (between 1.5kg threshold for recreational use), and a visual observer is required to maintain visual line of sight while the pilot uses FPV goggles.
Question 14
What should you do when there is a suspected loss of control link?
Activate return-to-home function
When there is a suspected loss of control link, the appropriate response is to activate the return-to-home (RTH) function if available. This automated function will bring the UA back to its designated home point safely. If RTH is not available, manual recovery procedures should be followed.
Question 20
What condition must be met under VLOS?
The pilot must be able to see the UA with unaided eyes
Visual Line of Sight (VLOS) requires that the pilot must be able to see the unmanned aircraft with unaided eyes (without the use of binoculars, telescopes, or other visual aids) at all times during flight operations. This ensures the pilot can maintain situational awareness and control of the aircraft.
Question 38
When you are operating a visual line of sight inspection of a fire scene and lose sight of your UA behind a plume of smoke, you should:
Immediately activate return-to-home function
When visual line of sight is lost during operations (such as when a UA disappears behind smoke), the pilot should immediately activate the return-to-home function to safely recover the aircraft. Maintaining VLOS is a critical safety requirement, and losing sight of the UA requires immediate corrective action.

Question 13
What are the two common errors for GNSS?
Multipath and atmospheric delay
The two most common errors affecting Global Navigation Satellite System (GNSS) accuracy are multipath errors (where signals bounce off surfaces before reaching the receiver) and atmospheric delay (where signals are delayed as they pass through the atmosphere, particularly the ionosphere and troposphere).
Question 19
What does RTK represent?
Real-Time Kinematic
RTK stands for Real-Time Kinematic, which is a satellite navigation technique used to enhance the precision of position data derived from satellite-based positioning systems. RTK can provide centimeter-level accuracy by using a fixed base station to provide correction data.
Question 47
What is the minimum number of satellites needed to make a connection?
4
A minimum of 4 satellites is needed to establish a GPS connection and determine a 3D position (latitude, longitude, and altitude). Three satellites can provide a 2D position, but the fourth satellite is necessary to calculate altitude and provide accurate timing information.
Question 18
How do you fix orientation problems if you are incapable of operating a UA in different nose directions?
Use headless mode
Headless mode (also called care-free mode) helps fix orientation problems by making the controls relative to the pilot's position rather than the aircraft's nose direction. In this mode, pushing the stick forward always moves the aircraft away from the pilot, regardless of which way the nose is pointing.
Question 23
Which of the following statements is wrong about Static Pressure?
Static pressure increases with altitude
The statement "Static pressure increases with altitude" is incorrect. Static pressure actually decreases with altitude. As you go higher in the atmosphere, there is less air above pressing down, so the atmospheric pressure decreases. This is why altimeters work - they measure the decrease in static pressure to determine altitude.
Question 24
Which of the following is a disadvantage of using an Ultrasound system?
Limited range
Ultrasound systems have limited range as a significant disadvantage. They typically work effectively only at very close distances (usually a few meters) and can be affected by environmental factors like wind, temperature, and surface texture. They are commonly used for precision landing and low-altitude hovering but are not suitable for long-range applications.
Question 45
Which of the following can be used to detect vibrations experienced by a UA?
Accelerometer
An accelerometer can detect vibrations experienced by a UA by measuring changes in acceleration along different axes. When the aircraft vibrates, it experiences rapid changes in acceleration that the accelerometer can detect and measure, making it useful for monitoring aircraft health and detecting mechanical issues.
Question 37
What is the maximum voltage of a fully charged 6-cell (6S) LiPo battery?
25.2V
A fully charged LiPo (Lithium Polymer) battery cell has a maximum voltage of 4.2V. Therefore, a 6-cell (6S) LiPo battery has a maximum voltage of 6 × 4.2V = 25.2V when fully charged. The nominal voltage would be 6 × 3.7V = 22.2V.
Question 40
Which of the following sources of information should you consult first when determining what maintenance should be performed on a UAS or its components?
Manufacturer's maintenance manual
The manufacturer's maintenance manual should be the first source of information consulted when determining maintenance requirements for a UAS or its components. This manual contains specific maintenance schedules, procedures, and requirements designed by the manufacturer for optimal performance and safety of the aircraft.
Question 44
When is there no need to change a propeller of a UA?
When there are minor scratches on the propeller surface
Minor scratches on the propeller surface that do not affect the structural integrity or aerodynamic performance do not require propeller replacement. However, significant damage such as cracks, chips, bent blades, or deep gouges would require immediate replacement for safety reasons.

Question 3
Which hazardous attitude best describes overestimating one's ability and underestimating the situation?
Macho
The "Macho" hazardous attitude is characterized by overestimating one's ability and underestimating the risks or complexity of a situation. Pilots with this attitude often take unnecessary risks, believing they can handle any situation regardless of the actual danger involved.
Question 10
Which of the following models describes decision-making?
DECIDE model
The DECIDE model is a structured decision-making process used in aviation. DECIDE stands for: Detect (the problem), Estimate (significance), Choose (course of action), Identify (best alternative), Do (take action), and Evaluate (monitor the solution). This systematic approach helps pilots make better decisions under pressure.
Question 17
The 5 decision-making models for risk assessment include:
PAVE, IMSAFE, DECIDE, 5P, 3P
The five decision-making models for risk assessment in aviation are: PAVE (Pilot, Aircraft, enVironment, External pressures), IMSAFE (Illness, Medication, Stress, Alcohol, Fatigue, Emotion), DECIDE (Detect, Estimate, Choose, Identify, Do, Evaluate), 5P (Plan, Plane, Pilot, Passengers, Programming), and 3P (Perceive, Process, Perform).
Question 22
Which of the following is not part of the CAAS risk assessment format?
Weather conditions
While weather conditions are an important safety consideration, they are not specifically part of the CAAS (Civil Aviation Authority of Singapore) risk assessment format structure. The CAAS risk assessment format typically focuses on specific operational risk categories and mitigation measures as defined by the regulatory framework.
Question 31
What is aptitude?
Natural ability to learn or understand
Aptitude refers to a person's natural ability to learn or understand something, especially a particular skill or subject. In aviation, pilot aptitude includes natural abilities in areas such as spatial awareness, hand-eye coordination, multitasking, and decision-making under pressure.
Question 39
Which of the following is considered a visual illusion?
False horizon
A false horizon is a visual illusion that occurs when the pilot mistakes other visual references (such as cloud formations, sloping terrain, or city lights) for the actual horizon. This can lead to spatial disorientation and incorrect aircraft attitude perception, making it a significant safety concern in aviation.

Key Concepts & Study Areas

Regulatory Framework

  • UAPL Classifications: Class A (up to 25kg) vs Class B (over 25kg)
  • Activity Permits: Class 1 (commercial) vs Class 2 (recreational/educational)
  • Weight Categories: Under 250g, 250g-1.5kg, 1.5kg-7kg, 7kg-25kg
  • Penalties: Dangerous flying ($100k/5 years), substance influence ($100k/5 years second offense)
  • Age Requirements: Minimum 16 for UAPL, supervised operation allowed for younger pilots

Aerodynamics & Performance

  • Stall Conditions: Occurs when critical angle of attack is exceeded
  • Density Altitude: High density altitude = poor performance, equals pressure altitude at 15°C
  • Center of Gravity: Forward CG = more stability, less maneuverability
  • Ground Effect: Occurs within ½ wingspan of ground
  • Control Axes: Pitch (lateral), Roll (longitudinal), Yaw (vertical)

Systems & Technology

  • Radio Control: Mode 1 vs Mode 2 transmitter configurations
  • GNSS Errors: Multipath and atmospheric delay
  • RTK: Real-Time Kinematic for centimeter-level accuracy
  • Battery Systems: LiPo cells = 4.2V max, 3.7V nominal
  • Sensors: Accelerometers detect vibrations, ultrasound has limited range

Safety & Operations

  • VLOS Requirements: Must see UA with unaided eyes at all times
  • Emergency Procedures: Activate RTH on control link loss
  • Decision Making: DECIDE, PAVE, IMSAFE, 5P, 3P models
  • Hazardous Attitudes: Macho (overconfidence), Anti-authority, Impulsivity, Invulnerability, Resignation
  • Visual Illusions: False horizon can cause spatial disorientation
