Question 7
How does a powered-lift pitch forward?

To increase speed forward or accelerate, the thrust must be greater than drag. Thrust overcomes drag for forward motion, while lift overcomes weight for vertical motion.

Important Concepts & Definitions

Principles of Flight

  • Four Forces of Flight: Lift (upward), Weight (downward), Thrust (forward), Drag (backward resistance)
  • Stall: Occurs when the critical angle of attack is exceeded, leading to loss of lift
  • Ground Effect: Reduced drag when flying less than one-half wingspan above ground
  • Vortex Ring State: Dangerous aerodynamic effect in rotorcraft causing instability and rapid descent

Air Law & Permits

  • Class A UAPL: Operates UA up to 25kg maximum weight
  • Class 1 AP: Required for non-recreational/non-educational purposes
  • Class 2 AP: Required for recreational/educational purposes in restricted areas
  • VLOS: Visual Line of Sight must be maintained at all times during flight
  • UABT Certificate: Required for drones 1.5kg-7kg for recreational use

Human Factors & Safety

  • DECIDE Model: Detect, Estimate, Choose, Identify, Do, Evaluate
  • Macho Attitude: Overestimating ability, underestimating situation
  • PAEOS Framework: Pilot, Aircraft, Environment, Operation, Situation
  • IMSAFE Checklist: Illness, Medication, Stress, Alcohol, Fatigue, Emotion

Technical Systems

  • GNSS Requirements: Minimum of 4 satellites needed for accurate positioning
  • LiPo Battery: Fully charged cell = 4.2V (6S battery = 25.2V)
  • RTK: Real-Time Kinematics for high-precision positioning
  • Frequency Hopping: Technique to reduce interference between transmitter/receiver
  • Binding: Process of pairing specific receiver with specific transmitter
